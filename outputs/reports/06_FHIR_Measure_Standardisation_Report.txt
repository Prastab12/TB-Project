SECTION A: LEARN FHIR STRUCTURE (CONCEPTUAL STUDY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We investigated the FHIR Clinical Reasoning Module structure to determine the most
interoperable format for public health monthly TB summaries.

1. Structural Strategy: Definitional vs Instance Resources
   - Measure (Definitional): Acts as the computable standard, defining groups,
     scoring methodologies, and populations.
   - MeasureReport (Instance): Holds the actual calculated results (numerator,
     denominator, and score) for a specific district over a specific month.

2. Scoring Types Enforced:
   - Proportion: Utilized when the numerator is a strict mathematical subset
     of the denominator. Score is bounded within [0.0 - 1.0].
   - Ratio: Utilized when the numerator is NOT a subset of the denominator 
     (e.g., gender count comparisons, or notification rates scaled per 100k).

3. Stratifiers:
   - Built-in SNOMED CT gender stratification schema (Code: 263495000) inside
     epidemiological groups to map demographic counts.
