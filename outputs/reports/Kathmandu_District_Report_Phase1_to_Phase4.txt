ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This report presents a district-specific account of the TB data integration
pipeline for Kathmandu District, Nepal — from raw data ingestion and quality
assessment through FHIR R4 transformation and system deployment. Over five
Bikram Sambat fiscal years (2078–2082), Kathmandu contributed 60 monthly
reporting records across 13 TB indicators. These records were assessed for
data quality, cleaned and standardised, architecturally mapped to HL7 FHIR R4
quality measure specifications, and ultimately transformed into 780 FHIR R4
MeasureReport resources exposed via a standards-conformant REST API and an
interactive public health dashboard.

Kathmandu District presents a clean, internally consistent dataset with no
over-reporting errors and a single structural missingness event confined to
three under-reported months in FY 2078. The annualised TB notification rate
for Kathmandu averaged 13.59 per 100,000 population across the reporting
period (excluding under-reported months), well below Nepal's high-burden
national average, reflecting the district's relatively robust TB case
detection and treatment infrastructure.

4. PHASE 3 — HL7 FHIR ARCHITECTURAL MAPPING (KATHMANDU)
================================================================================

4.1 Structural Design
----------------------
The FHIR Clinical Reasoning Module was selected as the interoperability
framework. Two FHIR R4 resource types form the mapping architecture:

  Measure (Definitional):
    A computable quality standard defining the indicator, scoring type,
    population criteria, and canonical URL. Measures are district-agnostic
    — they define what is measured, not where.

  MeasureReport (Instance):
    Holds the actual calculated result for Kathmandu District for a specific
    month. References the parent Measure by canonical URL. Contains the
    computed count values and scores.

4.2 Scoring Type Split
-----------------------
Two scoring types were applied based on the indicator's mathematical nature:

  RATIO Scoring — 7 Monthly Notification Indicators:
    Numerator   : TB case count (not a subset of the denominator)
    Denominator : CBS mid-year district population (not TB cohort)
    Score       : numerator / denominator (dimensionless; × 100,000 for rate)
    Populations : 2 per group (numerator + denominator)

  COHORT Scoring — 6 Annual Treatment Outcome Indicators:
    Population  : initial-population = pbc_reg (registered annual cohort)
    Score       : not calculated (counts only; rates derived in dashboard)
    Populations : 1 per group (initial-population)

4.3 Indicator Mapping Table
-----------------------------

  RATIO INDICATORS (Notification — Monthly):

  Measure ID                      | Title                      | Numerator Col
  ────────────────────────────────|────────────────────────────|─────────────────
  nepal-tb-new-cases-total        | New TB Cases (Total)       | new_cases_total
  nepal-tb-new-cases-female       | New TB Cases (Female)      | new_cases_female
  nepal-tb-new-cases-male         | New TB Cases (Male)        | new_cases_male
  nepal-tb-relapse-total          | TB Relapse Cases (Total)   | relapse_total
  nepal-tb-relapse-female         | TB Relapse Cases (Female)  | relapse_female
  nepal-tb-relapse-male           | TB Relapse Cases (Male)    | relapse_male
  nepal-tb-total-tb-notified      | Total TB Notified          | total_tb_m+f
  Denominator for all 7           :                              district_pop_mid_year_cbs

  COHORT INDICATORS (Treatment Outcomes — Annual):

  Measure ID                      | Title                      | Source Column
  ────────────────────────────────|────────────────────────────|─────────────────
  nepal-tb-pbc-reg                | PBC Registered Cohort      | pbc_reg
  nepal-tb-cured                  | TB Patients Cured          | cured
  nepal-tb-failed                 | TB Treatment Failures      | failed
  nepal-tb-died                   | TB Patients Died           | died
  nepal-tb-ltfu                   | TB Patients Lost to F/U    | ltfu

5.3 Data Absent Reason (DAR) Encoding
---------------------------------------
Under-reported months (BS 2078 Baishak, Jestha, Asar) and the single empty
cell (relapse_total BS 2079 Magh) are annotated using the HL7 FHIR R4
Data Absent Reason extension rather than carrying misleading zero values.

  Extension URL  : http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/data-absent-reason
  valueCode      : not-reported

  Applied to numerator population count:
    "_count": {
      "extension": [{
        "url": "http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/data-absent-reason",
        "valueCode": "not-reported"
      }]
    }

  Applied to measureScore:
    "measureScore": {
      "unit": "1", "system": "http://unitsofmeasure.org",
      "_value": {
        "extension": [{
          "url": "http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/data-absent-reason",
          "valueCode": "not-reported"
        }]
      }
    }

  DAR applied to         : All 7 ratio indicators for BS 2078 Baishak,
                           Jestha, Asar (21 MeasureReports)
  Additional DAR         : relapse-total BS 2079 Magh (1 MeasureReport)
  Total DAR annotations  : 22

  Note: The 6 cohort indicators (pbc_reg, cured, failed, died, ltfu,
  not_eval) carry valid annual values for all 60 months and are not
  affected by DAR encoding.

5.4 Bikram Sambat Calendar Extension
--------------------------------------
A custom FHIR extension was designed and applied to every MeasureReport
to preserve Nepal's Bikram Sambat calendar context within the FHIR Period
element:

  Extension canonical  : https://iihms.gov.np/fhir/StructureDefinition/nepali-fiscal-period
  Applied to           : MeasureReport.period (all 780 resources)
  Sub-extensions:
    bs-year      (valueInteger) : e.g., 2078
    bs-month     (valueString)  : e.g., "Shrawan"
    fiscal-year  (valueString)  : e.g., "2078/79"

  Fiscal year logic for Kathmandu:
    Shrawan–Chaitra : FY {bs_year}/{bs_year+1 mod 100}
    Baishak–Asar    : FY {bs_year-1}/{bs_year mod 100}

  This ensures every MeasureReport is simultaneously queryable by
  Gregorian date (for international systems) and BS date (for NTP and
  Nepali health analysts).

7. LIMITATIONS AND KNOWN GAPS
================================================================================

  1. Three Under-Reported Months (BS 2078 Baishak, Jestha, Asar):
     DHIS2 data was not collected for these periods. Handled via FHIR DAR
     encoding. FY 2078 notification totals represent only 9 of 12 months
     and are not directly comparable to subsequent full-year totals.

  2. One Isolated Missing Value (relapse_total, BS 2079 Magh):
     A single empty cell in the source data. Zero-filled in the final CSV
     and DAR-encoded in the corresponding MeasureReport.

  3. "Treatment Completed" Outcome Not in Source Schema:
     All 60 Kathmandu rows show sum of reported outcomes < pbc_reg. This
     is expected: the "Completed" treatment outcome category is absent from
     the DHIS2 export schema used for this project, not a data quality error.

  4. Annual Cohort Values Repeated Monthly:
     Treatment outcome counts (pbc_reg, cured, failed, died, ltfu, not_eval)
     are annual cohort figures. The same annual value appears in all 12
     monthly MeasureReports for that fiscal year. Monthly granularity for
     treatment outcomes is not available in the source data.

  5. FHIR Implementation Guide (Phase 5):
     The formal FHIR IG (FSH profiles, StructureDefinitions, GitHub Actions
     CI/CD, IG Publisher validation) is in progress and is not included in
     this report.

8. CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Kathmandu District's TB data integration pipeline has been completed across
all four phases with a 100% FHIR validation pass rate and zero data errors.

The district presents an exceptionally clean dataset: no over-reporting errors,
no negative values, complete ART and treatment outcome records, and missingness
confined entirely to a known structural reporting gap in BS 2078. This quality
profile made Kathmandu the appropriate choice as the primary FHIR transformation
target within the Bagmati Province dataset.

Epidemiologically, Kathmandu shows a positive trend: rising TB detection through
2080–2081, improving cure rates (reaching 87.1% in 2080), declining LTFU rates,
and a sharply falling "not evaluated" rate — all indicators of a maturing and
improving TB programme. The 51% growth in the registered treatment cohort between
2078 and 2081 reflects both population growth and improved case finding.

The 780 FHIR R4 MeasureReport resources generated for Kathmandu represent a
complete, interoperable, and semantically annotated TB performance record for
five fiscal years — ready for integration with national health information
exchange systems and international TB surveillance platforms.
